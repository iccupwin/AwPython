Paperclip Fitness

Project Description

Introduction

What is paperclip fitness?

Paperclip Fitness is a local startup that aims to A) provide college students useful information about their school sponsored recreational facility and B) aims to create a social hub within the recreational facility, letting students connect to each through the use of a mobile app.

Problem

The current app is in it’s beta stage. Robert would like us to give him design recommendations on how the app should look, that is backed up by of findings in validation tests, interviews, surveys, academic articles, and professional judgement.

Initial Usability Testing

Procedure

We created a usability protocol that asks the user their first initial thoughts and impressions of the app, what its purpose is, followed by three tasks. In the end, we asked them to share their overall experience. Each team member conducted two usability tests on students who are college students that are familiar with the Co-Rec.

Results

Users struggled with the following:

  • 1. Identifying what the app does
  • 2. Understanding or recognizing the language that is being used by the app
  • 3. Layout and navigation of the app
  • 4. Unable to find areas within the Co-rec
  • 5. Thought the design was kind of plain
  • 6. Lack of labels

    • Heuristic Evaluation

    What is a heuristic evaluation?

    Heuristics are Jakob Nielsen’s broad rules of thumbs for design. There are a total of 10 heuristics that should be considered when designing.

    The Heuristics

  • 1. Visibility of system status
  • 2. Match between system and the real world
  • 3. User control and freedom
  • 4. Consistency and standards
  • 5. Error prevention
  • 6. Recognition rather than recall
  • 7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
  • 8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
  • 9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
  • 10. Help and documentation

    • *The bolded items will be the heuristics we will see in the Paperclip App. These will be defined, for simplicity, we will leave out the four heuristics that were either not violated or not evident in the app.

    Heuristic Procedure

    Each team member individually went through the heuristics and looked for troublesome areas that were mentioned previously by users in the usability test. We then tried to match the potential issues with a correlating heuristic. Severity was rated based on the frequency, persistent, and impact on usability. We did not do an evaluation on the feedback or report bug page as it is not something that would entice the users. In other words, it’s not the primarily part of the app.

    Severity Rating

    Severity is rated based on a 0-4 scale. The rating number is based on frequency, persistence, and impact. It gives us a way to classify the heuristics and how severely they are violated. For instances where the severity was 0, we left it out because it does not indicate a problem.

    Interviews

    Purpose

    By conducting interviews, we hoped to find some underlying problems that students may be facing. As well as look for some factors that either motivate or preventing them from going to the Co-rec. The information collected here also helps us develop personas, which will be talked about later on.

    Results

    From our interviews here are some of the common traits/characteristics/behaviors we’ve noticed:

  • 1. Everyone has a workout routine, or at least favorite spots within the corec that they go to often
  • 2. No one was really familiar with the layout of the corec
  • 3. Many of the potential users are focused towards working out rather than social activities
  • 4. There are few frustrations with the corec overall with the exception of re-racking weights, and overcrowdedness
  • 5. Students really like the corec due to it’s size, availability, and range of equipment

    Pivot

    During the 15th week of our project, we met with Robert to go over Personas. It was at this time, Robert had told us of his decision to not partnering up with the Co-rec at this time. He wanted Paperclip Fitness to be a standalone app that would provide value and benefits to the patrons on it’s own. This meant some changes for us too… We were not longer bounded by Purdue Branding guides, this meant that we have to create a separate style guide on how the app might look. In order to provide the best value to the patrons, Robert needed to incorporate more features into the app. This meant that we would have to redesign the structure of the app in order to include several more features instead of the original “Call a Fitcon” and “Facility Usage”.

    New features

    While we were conducting our interviews, Robert has been doing interviews of his own. He interviewed a total of 60 Purdue Students and here are some of the features they mentioned:

  • 1. Facility Usage: Letting the patrons know how many people are in a specific area of the Core
  • 2. Navigation: A way for patrons to find where a specific area is located
  • 3. Open Rec Queueing: Lets patrons create events or activities for others to join and participate
  • 4. Workout Library: A library of different comprehensive workouts, such has toning deltoids...etc
  • 5. Workout Tracker: To track calories, how many sets/repetitions, distance walked/ran...etc
  • 6. Information System: Information about the Co-rec and corresponding activities/clubs


    • We were granted access to a SurveyMonkey account courtesy of Robert. We designed our survey to be short and simple. We presented the six features along with a short description of their functionalities. We then asked the participants to rank them from 1 to 6. 1 would be the most desirable and 6 being the least desirable.



    Out of 26 responses, the top three choices for the most desirable features for an app such as Paperclip were:
    1. Facility Usage
    2. Information System
    3. Navigation

    • Color Theory

    Smashing Magazine Suggests


  • Red - Orange - Yellow: Are seen as passionate, energetic, and positive.
  • Green - Blue - Purple: Are seen as calming, relaxing, and reserved.
  • Black - White - Gray - Beige: Are seen as sophisticaed, formality, and simple.

    • The warmer colors of red,orange, and yellow is exactly what Robert wants. We know that typically reds, in the Western world, tend to represent STOP or otherwise negative connotations. Because of this, we will most likely leave out red but use primarily the orange and yellow to get that energetic feel Robert is looking for.
